# Customer Churn Analysis (Excel Project)

This project analyzes customer churn for a telecom company using Excel.  
It explores why customers leave, identifies the major drivers of churn, and visualizes key insights using pivot tables and charts.

---

## Project Objectives
- Understand the factors contributing to customer churn  
- Analyze customer behavior across contract types, payment methods, and tenure  
- Identify high-risk customer groups  
- Provide actionable recommendations to reduce churn

## Key Insights
-	**Month–to–month customers have the highest churn rate** contributing **23.50%** of the **26.54%** total churn rate.
-	**Customers without tech support are 3x more likely to churn** than customers with tech support.
-	**Fiber optic has the highest churn rate** of **18.42%** compared to other internet types.
-	**New customers of Tenure <12 months** churn higher, indicating poor early onboarding.
-	**Electronic check payment users** churn the most compared to other payment users, suggesting payment friction or financial instability.
- **Customers with high monthly charges** churn more, either service dissatisfaction or financial strain.

## Recommendation
-	**Offer discounts or loyalty points** for customers who commit to annual renewals to encourage long-term retention.
-	**Streamline the onboarding process** by simplifying steps and offering digital self-service options to improve the first customer experience.
-	**Ensure hassle-free installation** by minimizing delays and providing clear setup instructions or technician support.
-	**Introduce flexible pricing plans** for customers paying over $80/month to better match their needs and increase satisfaction.
-	**Promote automatic payments** to reduce late payments and improve billing consistency, which can help lower churn rates. This is especially important because customers using electronic checks show a higher tendency to churn compared to those using automated payment methods.
-	**Make billing user-friendly and transparent** to reduce confusion, disputes, and frustration.
-	**Enhance technical support services** by reducing wait times and improving resolution quality to boost customer confidence.
-	**Proactively address technical issues** that commonly lead to churn, such as service outages or connectivity problems.
